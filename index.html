About Me

Imagination creates reality--Richard Wagner

Oscar was born in San Antonio, TX and grew up in Laredo, TX. He began learning how to play guitar at the age of 10 from his father and then learned bass guitar at age 11 where he was in his first garage band with his school friends. He then joined the middle school band in eighth grade and picked the french horn. After earning his high school diploma, he began working on his Bachelor of Music at Texas A&M University â€“ Kingsville. While a horn and bass music student, he performed with the TAMUK Horn Choir, Q1 Horn Quartet, Jazz Band and Jazz Combos in the U.S. including: Texas, Alabama, Illinois, Colorado and abroad in Germany and Switzerland for the 41st International Horn Symposium. He was also a string bassist in 2006 with the TAMUK Wind Symphony at the Texas Music Educators Association in San Antonio, TX.

After graduation from TAMUK, Oscar earned a position from La Joya ISD as Head Orchestra Director where he taught guitar and orchestra classes from 2010-2014. In 2014, he then began working at Morris MS as an Associate Band Director where he taught concert band, marching band, jazz band, and brass beginners. As an educator, his students were regular members of the TMEA Region XV Band/Orchestra, TMEA XV Jazz Bands, and were superior performers at UIL State Solo & Ensemble and UIL Concert & Sight-Reding contests. Outside of work, Oscar performed regularly on horn with the McAllen Wind Ensemble and bass guitar with the RGV Swing Street Big Band.

Currently Oscar is working to acquire skills as a full stack web developer from UTSA and is enjoying the ride of new opportunities every day.
